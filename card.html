The koala has the most effective insulating back fur of any marsupial and is highly resilient to wind and rain, while the belly fur can reflect solar radiation.

  • Scientific Name: Phascolarctos Cinereus
  • Conservation Status: Vulnerable
  • Average Length: 60-85cm
  • Trophic level: Herbivorous

The koala is a cute animal with a large head and non existent tail. These animals are mostly found in Australia, has a body length of 60–85 cm (24–33 in) and a weight of 4–15 kg. Koalas from Victoria are twice as heavy as those from Queensland, The curved and sharp claws are well adapted for climbing trees.
